Navient Complaints Soar as Borrowers Resume Repayment

CFPB data shows sharp increases in complaints in 2024 and 2025, years after major lawsuits and settlements.

By Your Name | JOUR352

Complaints filed against Navient surged over the past decade, rising sharply in the years following major federal and state legal actions against the student loan servicer. CFPB data shows a steady climb in annual complaints from 2012 through 2017, the year the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau sued Navient for steering borrowers into long-term forbearance and mishandling payments. Complaints dipped slightly in 2018 and 2019, but the numbers climbed again during the pandemic and remained elevated after a 2022 settlement requiring the company to provide $1.85 billion in relief.

The steepest increases came in the last three years. Complaints grew from about 49,000 in 2023 to more than 79,000 in 2024 and nearly doubled again in 2025. The spike aligns with the end of the federal student loan payment pause and renewed scrutiny of loan servicers as millions of borrowers returned to repayment. These trends suggest widespread frustration remains despite settlements and promises of reform. For more background on the lawsuits, see Student Loan Planner or visit Navient.com.
